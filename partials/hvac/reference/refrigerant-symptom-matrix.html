Purpose: Use pressure/temperature patterns as a triage tool, then confirm with SH/SC, airflow, and electrical data.

Observed Pattern Likely Causes First Checks Do Not Do
Low suction + low head Undercharge, evaporator starvation, low load Leak search, SH/SC, airflow baseline, indoor load context Do not add charge blindly without leak/airflow verification
Low suction + high head Liquid line restriction, metering restriction, severe airflow issue Filter/coil/static, temp drop across restriction points, subcool trend Do not condemn compressor first
High suction + low head Compressor inefficiency, valve damage, bypassing Compression ratio, amp draw trend, discharge temp behavior Do not assume overcharge
High suction + high head Overcharge, non-condensables, heavy load, condenser airflow degradation Condenser coil/fan, SC trend, ambient/load context, recovery history Do not recover charge before confirming airflow/load conditions
Normal pressures, poor cooling Airflow/distribution issue, duct leakage, mixed air, control fault TESP/static split, blower profile, delta-T location method, duct inspection Do not force refrigerant adjustment to fix comfort complaint
Head pressure climbs over time Condenser airflow collapse, fouled coil, fan cycling issue, non-condensables Fan amps/capacitor, coil cleanliness, ambient rise, SC trend Do not keep unit running hard without protection checks

How to Use This Matrix Correctly

  1. Stabilize operating conditions and verify airflow first.
  2. Capture full data set: pressures, line temps, SH, SC, Delta-T, static, amps.
  3. Match observed pattern to likely causes.
  4. Run one confirming test per likely cause.
  5. Repair only after confirmation, then re-test and document final readings.

Quick Tips (New + Veteran Techs)

  • One odd reading is noise; trend over several minutes is signal.
  • Airflow faults can mimic charge faults almost perfectly.
  • Always confirm refrigerant type before PT interpretation.
  • Use the same probe placement each visit to keep data comparable.

Matrix is a decision accelerator, not a replacement for confirmatory testing.
