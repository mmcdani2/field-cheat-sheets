Purpose: Delta-T is a fast performance check, not a standalone diagnosis.

What Delta-T Is

Cooling Delta-T = Return Air Dry Bulb - Supply Air Dry Bulb

Heating Delta-T = Supply Air Dry Bulb - Return Air Dry Bulb

Field Procedure (Cooling Mode)

  1. Set thermostat to call for cooling and let system run 10–15 minutes to stabilize.
  2. Confirm basics first: clean filter, blower running, coil not visibly iced, all major registers open.
  3. Measure return air dry bulb at return grille or return plenum (avoid attic air influence).
  4. Measure supply air dry bulb a few feet downstream of coil (or nearest main supply trunk).
  5. Calculate: Delta-T = Return - Supply.
  6. Record outdoor temp, indoor temp/humidity, static pressure, and Delta-T together for context.

Quick Interpretation (Cooling)

  • ~16–22°F: Common range on many properly operating systems under typical load.
  • Low Delta-T (<~16°F): Often high airflow, low charge, duct leakage, high latent load, or mixed air issues.
  • High Delta-T (>~22°F): Often low airflow (dirty filter/coil, blower issues, duct restriction) or low indoor load.

Manufacturer airflow and charging targets override generic ranges. Use Delta-T as a screening KPI, not final proof.

How-To Workflow (No Guessing)

  1. Step 1: Airflow first. Check filter, blower speed/tap, wheel cleanliness, evaporator condition, and TESP.
  2. Step 2: Refrigerant second. Verify charge by SH/SC method matched to metering device.
  3. Step 3: Envelope/load context. Check doors/windows, duct leakage, return infiltration, indoor RH.
  4. Step 4: Re-test and trend. Capture before/after Delta-T and include in job notes/photos.

Quick Tips (Veteran + New Tech)

  • Use the same measurement locations every visit for apples-to-apples trend data.
  • Don’t read right off the coil face or at one supply register only—use representative points.
  • If humidity is high, sensible split can look lower even when system is doing real latent work.
  • Pair Delta-T with static pressure + SH/SC + amp draw before quoting major repairs.
  • After any adjustment (charge/airflow), allow stabilization before taking final readings.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Diagnosing refrigerant issues from Delta-T alone.
  • Skipping airflow verification before charging adjustments.
  • Taking readings too early (no stabilization time).
  • Using different probe locations each time and comparing as if trend data is valid.

Closeout standard: log Delta-T with return/supply temps, ambient, static, and charging metrics. That’s defensible diagnostics.
