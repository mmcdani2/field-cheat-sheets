Purpose: Charge and diagnose fixed-orifice/piston systems using measured superheat versus target.

When to Use

  • Indoor coil uses a fixed metering device (not TXV/EEV controlling evaporator feed).
  • You need to evaluate underfeed/overfeed risk at the evaporator.
  • Airflow has already been verified as acceptable.

How-To

  1. Confirm refrigerant type and system metering style.
  2. Measure suction pressure at outdoor service port.
  3. Convert suction pressure to suction saturation temp (PT chart/app).
  4. Measure suction line temperature on insulated section near condenser service area.
  5. Calculate: Superheat = Suction Line Temp - Suction Saturation Temp.
  6. Compare measured SH to target SH chart (typically indoor WB + outdoor DB inputs).
  7. Adjust refrigerant in small increments only, then re-stabilize and re-check.

Quick Tips

  • Clamp placement and insulation over probe matter—bad placement gives fake SH.
  • Do not evaluate charge until airflow/static/filter/coil are baseline-correct.
  • Record ambient and indoor load conditions so readings are defensible later.
  • Trend data for several minutes; don’t react to one transient reading.

Common Failures

Symptom Likely Cause First Check
Superheat high vs target Undercharge, evaporator starvation, low indoor load misread Leak indicators, airflow baseline, compare with subcool trend
Superheat low vs target Overfeed/overcharge, low load, floodback risk Compressor return conditions, suction temp stability, ambient context
Superheat unstable/wild Probe placement issue, intermittent airflow, unstable load Re-seat/insulate probe, verify blower consistency, watch trend over time
“Normal SH” but poor comfort Distribution/duct/control issue, latent load problem Static pressure, Delta-T method, duct leakage/mixed air checks

Field rule: On fixed-orifice systems, superheat is a charging metric only after airflow and load sanity checks are complete.
